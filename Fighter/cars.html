First Generation Fighter Aircraft

The first generation warplanes, which started to enter the inventory of the country's air force in the 1940s, could cruise at subsonic speeds with their jet engines. First generation jet fighter aircraft had little difference in appearance, namely in design, from the piston models produced in the same years and used by the air force.

Second Generation Fighter Aircraft

They are jet aircraft developed in the period from the mid-1950s to the early 1960s. These aircraft were developed taking into account the needs of the nuclear defense strategy. The use of aluminum alloy in aircraft, delta wings and backward swept wings were seen for the first time in this period.

Third Generation Fighter Aircraft

They are jet planes developed in the period from the beginning of the 1960s to about 1970. In this period, there were remarkable innovations in maneuverability and air-to-ground attack capabilities. The steam gauges used in the cockpits have started to be replaced by analog gauges. Technologies for vertical/short take-off and landing purposes have started to be used.

Fourth Generation Fighter Jet

In this period, when fourth generation jet aircraft were developed to be used in multiple roles, jet aircraft began to be equipped with very complex avionic systems and weapons. A very effective maneuverability was also achieved in this period. Analog avionic systems, on the other hand, started to give way to digital avionics systems in this period.
